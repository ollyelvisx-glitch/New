28 Fundamental Beliefs

1. The Doctrine of God
1. The Holy Scriptures

The Bible is the inspired, infallible word of God—the ultimate rule of faith.

2 Timothy 3:16, 2 Peter 1:20-21
2. The Trinity

There is one God: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—a unity of three co-eternal Persons.

Matthew 28:19, 2 Corinthians 13:14
3. The Father

God the Eternal Father is the Creator, Source, Sustainer, and Sovereign of all creation.

John 3:16, 1 John 4:8
4. The Son

God the Eternal Son became incarnate in Jesus Christ. Through Him, all things were created, and He revealed God's character.

John 1:1-3, 14, Colossians 1:15-19
5. The Holy Spirit

God the Eternal Spirit was active with the Father and the Son in Creation, Incarnation, and Redemption, and draws people to God.

Genesis 1:1-2, Luke 1:35, John 16:7-13
2. The Doctrine of Humanity

This covers where we came from, who we are, and how sin broke the world.

6. Creation

God is Creator of all things, and has revealed in Scripture the authentic, historical account of His creative activity in a literal six-day week.

Genesis 1-2, Exodus 20:8-11
7. Nature of Humanity

Man and woman were made in the image of God with individuality, power, and freedom to think and do. Sin entered when they disobeyed, fracturing that image.

Genesis 1:26-28, Romans 5:12-17
3. The Doctrine of Salvation

How Jesus rescues humanity from the penalty of sin.

8. The Great Controversy

All humanity is now involved in a cosmic conflict between Christ and Satan regarding the character of God, His law, and His sovereignty.

Revelation 12:4-9, Isaiah 14:12-14
9. Life, Death, and Resurrection of Christ

In Christ's life of perfect obedience to God's will, His suffering, death, and resurrection, God provided the only means of atonement for human sin.

John 3:16, Romans 5:8, 1 Corinthians 15:3-4
10. Experience of Salvation

In infinite love and mercy God made Christ, who knew no sin, to be sin for us, so that in Him we might be made the righteousness of God.

2 Corinthians 5:17-21, Ephesians 2:4-10
11. Growing in Christ

By His death on the cross Jesus triumphed over the forces of evil. Through the Holy Spirit, we are delivered from the past and empowered to grow spiritually.

Colossians 1:13-14, Galatians 2:20
4. The Doctrine of the Church

The identity, unity, and mission of God's people on earth.

12. The Church

The church is the community of believers who confess Jesus Christ as Lord and Saviour.

Ephesians 1:22-23, Matthew 16:13-18
13. The Remnant and Its Mission

In the last days, a remnant is called out to keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus, announcing the arrival of the judgment hour.

Revelation 12:17, Revelation 14:6-12
14. Unity in the Body of Christ

The church is one body with many members, called from every nation, kindred, tongue, and people, equal in Christ.

Romans 12:4-5, Galatians 3:27-29
15. Baptism

By baptism we confess our faith in the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ, and testify of our death to sin and of our purpose to walk in newness of life. Done by full immersion.

Matthew 28:19-20, Romans 6:1-6
16. The Lord’s Supper

Participation in the emblems of the body and blood of Jesus as an expression of faith in Him. Prefaced by the ordinance of foot-washing.

1 Corinthians 11:23-26, John 13:1-17
17. Spiritual Gifts and Ministries

God bestows upon all members of His church in every age spiritual gifts, which each member is to employ in loving ministry.

Romans 12:4-8, Ephesians 4:11-16
18. The Gift of Prophecy

One of the identifying gifts of the remnant church is the gift of prophecy, manifested in the ministry of Ellen G. White.

Joel 2:28-29, Revelation 19:10
5. The Doctrine of the Christian Life

How an Adventist lives out their faith on a daily basis.

19. Law of God

The great principles of God's law are embodied in the Ten Commandments and exemplified in the life of Christ. They express God's love, will, and purposes.

Exodus 20:1-17, Matthew 5:17-20
20. The Sabbath

The beneficent Creator, after the six days of Creation, rested on the seventh day and instituted the Sabbath for all people as a perpetual memorial of Creation.

Genesis 2:1-3, Exodus 20:8-11, Luke 4:16
21. Stewardship

We are God's stewards, entrusted by Him with time and opportunities, abilities and possessions, and the blessings of the earth and its resources. We return tithes and offerings.

Malachi 3:8-12, 1 Corinthians 9:9-14
22. Christian Behavior

We are called to be a godly people who think, feel, and act in harmony with biblical principles in all aspects of personal and social life, including physical health.

Romans 12:1-2, 1 Corinthians 10:31
23. Marriage and the Family

Marriage was divinely instituted in Eden and affirmed by Jesus to be a lifelong union between a man and a woman in loving companionship.

Genesis 2:18-25, Matthew 19:3-9
6. The Doctrine of Last Things (Eschatology)

Prophecy, judgment, death, and the ultimate restoration of the earth.

24. Christ’s Ministry in the Heavenly Sanctuary

There is a sanctuary in heaven, the true tabernacle that the Lord set up. In it, Christ ministers on our behalf, making available the benefits of His atoning sacrifice.

Hebrews 8:1-5, Daniel 8:14
25. Second Coming of Christ

The second coming of Christ is the blessed hope of the church, the grand climax of the gospel. It will be literal, personal, visible, and worldwide.

Titus 2:13, Matthew 24, Revelation 1:7
26. Death and Resurrection

The wages of sin is death. But God, who alone is immortal, will grant eternal life to His redeemed. Until that day, death is an unconscious state for all people.

Ecclesiastes 9:5-6, 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18
27. Millennium and the End of Sin

The millennium is the thousand-year reign of Christ with His saints in heaven between the first and second resurrections. During this time, the wicked dead are judged.

Revelation 20, 1 Corinthians 6:2-3
28. New Earth

On the new earth, in which righteousness dwells, God will provide an eternal home for the redeemed and a perfect environment for everlasting life, love, joy, and learning.

2 Peter 3:13, Revelation 21:1-7
